The fashion industry has become the second most polluting industry in the world after oil production. Fast fashion

The fashion industry is the second largest polluter in the world. 20% of industrial water pollution stems from textiles development, fashion industry being also responsible for emitting 1.2 billions tonnes of greenhouse gas emmissions a year. By 2050, it is estimated that clothing production will account for 25% of the world carbon budget. That's more than transport and agriculture.

The problem isn't what's available to buy, it's the throwaway culture of continuously buying new garments. So, what's the cause? Fast fashion.

Sustainability reaches the mass market

Mass market fashion retailers such as Zara and H&M hava launched sustainable and ethical clothing collections.

H&M claims their Conscious Collection is ethically sourced and uses recyled and organic materials. Their recyling initiative offers customers vouchers for recyling old clothes, this initiative influences customer's behaviour of further consumptions, relying on the fast fashion model, is something we may think. However, H&M can be praised for taking a step in the right direction beginning with their sustainable clothes and raising awareness of the worrying side effects of our shopping addictions.

Inditex Group, the owner of Zara, is the largest fashion company in the world, meaning they have a huge potential to reduce our environmental impacts. They've been ranked as the most sustainable fashion company by Dow Jones Sustainability Index. The company's goal is to develop an efficient life cycle for their clothes, meaning less textile landfill waste. They begun taking steps by reusing unused textiles. Like H&M Conscious, Zara have started using recycled materials and ecologically grown cotton rough the Zara Join Life range, atrendy and reasonably priced collection which has significantly grown in popularity since launching in 2016.
